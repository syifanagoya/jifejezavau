    Hang Curtains High

    Hanging the drapes high makes the window and the room seem taller. If you have crown moulding, mount the curtain rod right beneath the crown moulding. If the window is less than 6” from the ceiling, and there is no crown moulding, then ceiling mounting the drapes is preferable.

    Herein, can you hang curtains within the window frame?

    Generally, hanging curtain brackets on the wall above and outside the window moulding looks best; it enables cloth to fall smoothly. If you have elaborate window frames you don’t want to cover, an inside mount (hanging curtains inside the frame, as you would with a tension rod) may work.

    One may also wonder, where should drapery rods be placed?

    The higher the curtain rod, the taller the window will look, therefore place your curtain rod closer to the ceiling than the top of your window, if you can. One rule of thumb is that they should sit 4-6 inches above the window frame.

    In relation to this, how far above window should you hang curtains?

    Rule 1: Hang Curtains Above the Frame To give the idea of a taller window, install the rod four to six inches above the window frame—or midway between the frame and the ceiling moulding. (But within reason—don’t go more than eight inches over the frame or it can appear weird.)
