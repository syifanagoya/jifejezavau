    Early Life & Biography

    Shannon Emery Lee was born in Los Angeles to Bruce Lee, who was a film legend and Linda Lee Caldwell, who worked as a martial arts teacher. Her family moved to Hong Kong when she was young, and she spent most of her childhood there before the family returning to the United States.  She has an elder brother Brandon Lee who is also an actor and a martial artist.

    Shannon went to school in the United States, and for her higher learning, she went to Tulane University, where she took voice as a major. In her youth, she studied the martial art that was created by her father called Jeet Kune Do. She also studied Taekwondo and Wushu under different instructors. For the film Enter the Eagles, she was trained by kickboxing by Benny Urquidez.

    Career

    The professional career of Shannon Lee started in the 1990s, and she made her debut appearance in a 1993 film by the title of Dragon: The Bruce Lee Story, where she was a part singer of the song “California Dreamin.” The following year she appeared in another film called Cage 11, where she appeared with Lou Ferrigno, who is a veteran bodybuilder and an actor.

    In 1997 she had a role in the film High Voltage with Antonio Sabato Jr. She later starred in the film Enter the Eagles, which was a Hong Kong action movie that co-starred Anita Yuen and Michael Wong, and it was directed by Corey Yuen.

    Apart from appearing in various films, Lee has also appeared in different television series like Martial Law (1998), and Epoch in 2001. She landed a lead role in a 2001 action film titled Lessons for an Assassin. By now, she had become very famous, and many people were getting to like her more.

    She was chosen as the host of the first season of the WMAC game show. In 2003 she sang on the album of the band called Medicine, and the album was titled The Mechanical Forces of Love. She was the one who sang the cover song “I’m in the Mood for Love” for the 2000 film China Strike Force.

The series is based on the life of her father, Bruce Lee. She as well produced the documentary film called How Bruce Lee Changed the World, which was released in 2009.

    In 2019 the television series Warrior made a debut on Cinemax. This was a series that was based on an idea by Bruce Lee, and Shannon was the producer of the series, where she worked with Perfect Storm Entertainment and the famous filmmaker Justin Lin.
